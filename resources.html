Resources

The following list of resources are some of the many things that our club uses to improve!

General

The website by the National Academic Quizbowl Tournament has a lot of general information on what quizbowl is and how to get started.

The quizbowl forums give a lot of information about general advice and tournament updates.

Alternatively, you may check the hsquizbowl or DMV discord servers to communicate with other competitors.

Getting Better

There are a lot of quizbowl databases, the most prominent being QuizDB and aseemsDB. These databases give questions from past tournaments which you can use to learn. For more information, you can see William's guide to QuizDB.

For practice, you may try protobowl to compete against other players and simulate past questions. The hsquizbowl packet archive has a bunch of packets from past tournaments which you can read.

For NAQT tournaments, the NAQT You Gotta Know section has a lot of information on different clues you may get. You can also listen to past HSNCT matches here.

To memorize clues, you can use flashcards. The best places to do this online are Anki and Quizlet.

Also, Wikipedia exists.
